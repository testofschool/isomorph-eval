\begin{abstract}
The credibility of AI evaluation is threatened by two compounding
failures: benchmark contamination (test items leak into training data)
and naive aggregation (simple averaging ignores item difficulty and data
sparsity). We present \textbf{Isomorph-Eval}, a unified framework that
addresses both. We formalize \emph{$\tau$-isomorphism}, a graph-theoretic
definition of structural equivalence between benchmark items, and build
an open-source \emph{Isomorphic Engine} that generates unlimited
verified variants of existing benchmark items with automatically computed
answers. We define the \emph{Contamination Delta}
$\Delta_{\mathrm{contam}}^{\mathrm{IRT}}$, an IRT-weighted metric
grounded in Differential Item Functioning, that quantifies memorization
by comparing model performance on original items versus structurally
equivalent variants. In controlled simulations, the IRT-weighted delta
is 23$\times$ more discriminative than raw accuracy gaps. Applied to
GSM8K with five LLMs (including a negative control), evaluation on
59 independently verified items shows that four of five models exhibit
no significant contamination ($\Delta \in [+0.049, +0.066]$, all
$p > 0.05$), while one model shows a mild gap
($\Delta = +0.126$, $p = 0.002$). Critically, the framework uncovered
a data quality confound in annotation-chain replay that inflated
apparent contamination signals uniformly across all models, demonstrating
the importance of independent answer verification in benchmark
construction. We release the complete pipeline as open-source software.
\end{abstract}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented Isomorph-Eval, a unified framework for detecting
benchmark contamination while correcting for data sparsity and item
difficulty heterogeneity. The framework makes three contributions:
(1)~$\tau$-isomorphism formalizes structural equivalence between
benchmark items via graph-theoretic invariants; (2)~the Isomorphic
Engine generates unlimited verified variants with automatically computed
answers; and (3)~the Contamination Delta $\Dcont$ provides an
IRT-grounded diagnostic that is 23$\times$ more discriminative than
raw accuracy gaps in controlled simulations.

Our empirical evaluation on GSM8K with five models revealed an
important methodological lesson: initial results suggested uniform
contamination across all models ($\Delta \approx +0.42$), but
independent answer verification identified broken variant answers in
40\% of items. On the 59 verified-clean items, the contamination
signal collapsed to $\Delta \approx +0.05$--$0.13$, with only
Llama~3.1 8B showing a mild but significant gap. This finding
underscores that \emph{answer verification must be independent of the
generation pipeline}---a principle that applies to any benchmark
perturbation methodology.

The $\tau$-isomorphism framework and the Isomorphic Engine are the
lasting contributions of this work. They provide a principled
methodology for generating structurally equivalent benchmark variants
that can be applied to any binary-scored evaluation task with
decomposable reasoning structure. We release the complete pipeline
as open-source software at
\url{https://github.com/testofschool/isomorph-eval}.

\paragraph{Future work.}
Three directions are priorities: (1)~fixing the annotation-chain
replay bugs and regenerating variants for the full dataset to increase
statistical power; (2)~extending the framework beyond mathematical
reasoning to logic, code generation, and scientific reasoning;
(3)~fitting full 2PL IRT models with $\geq 30$ models to enable
proper item parameter estimation and DIF testing.
